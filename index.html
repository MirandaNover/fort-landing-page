How Fort Works

Move beyond heart rate monitoring with advanced sensors that measure muscle activity and fatigue.

Fort combines muscle sensors (EMG) and motion sensors (IMU) in a small wearable. It measures how your muscles fire, how deep you squat, how fast you lift, and how your body responds. It records contractions, range of motion, rep speed, stability, oxygenation, heart rate, and fatigue, then fuses those signals into meaningful metrics like neuromuscular efficiency, velocity loss, and form consistency. These metrics are compared against exercise-specific profiles of good technique to flag short range, rushed eccentrics, momentum, or breakdown. Finally, Fort translates everything into simple and actionable feedback with clear cues, fatigue markers, and progress trends so you can train smarter, protect your form, and see measurable improvement over time.
